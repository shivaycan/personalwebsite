The City as a Courtyard

Walking as a method, mapping attention to thresholds and light.

I have started treating the city like a courtyard. Not a thoroughfare, but a sequence of thresholds. Each street becomes a corridor; each intersection is a small shrine to choice. The purpose of the walk is not to arrive, but to notice the quiet architecture between arrivals.

My favorite routes are the ones that pass through contrast: shadow to light, silence to traffic, rough stone to polished glass. The body keeps time and the mind keeps score. When both agree, the walk becomes a form of writing.

A courtyard teaches you to listen before entering. The city can do the same, if you allow pauses to be part of the journey. I carry a small notebook and write one sentence at each pause. By the end of the day, the city has become a paragraph.

The older I get, the more I value the gates. The brief moments where you can decide to step inside or keep moving. Those gates are where the work begins.
