Letters to a Future Workshop

Writing to a future self about the studio that would make the work honest.

Dear future studio, I hope you are small enough to hear everything. I hope you still have a place for morning light and the slow rhythm of making. I hope the tools are modest and the work is precise.

When you arrive, remember that the workshop is a promise, not a trophy. It should keep the work legible and keep the ego quiet. It should smell of wood and tea, and it should never be too busy to learn.

Leave one wall unfinished. Let it remind you that the best rooms are still becoming. Let it make you generous. Let it keep your ambition honest.
