date: "2026-03-09"
tags:

The internet rewards speed, noise, and novelty.
Real engineering rewards clarity, patience, and repeatability.

I want my work to be measured less by announcements,
and more by whether it survives real use.
Can it be maintained?
Can someone else understand it in six months?
Can it fail safely?

Good software is often quiet.
It feels obvious only after someone has done the hard thinking.

My rule right now is simple:
build slower than hype, but stronger than trend.
