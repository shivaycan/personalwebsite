A thought keeps returning to me:
why does one visible tragedy move the world,
while thousands of invisible losses pass like numbers on a screen?

Some grief is amplified by media,
and some grief is buried by distance.
But distance should not decide dignity.

When outrage becomes selective,
we slowly normalize imbalance.
The death of one should matter.
The death of many should matter more, not less.

I write this as a reminder to myself:
attention is moral power,
and where we place it shapes the kind of society we become.
